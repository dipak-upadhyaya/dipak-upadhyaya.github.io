About

Trustworthy, clinically meaningful AI for healthcare.

I am a Postdoctoral Fellow in Biomedical and Health Informatics in the Department of Population and Quantitative Health Sciences at Case Western Reserve University, where I work with Dr. Satya Sahoo. My research focuses on developing trustworthy, explainable, and clinically meaningful artificial intelligence methods for healthcare.

My work sits at the intersection of machine learning, large language models, explainable AI, and clinical decision support. I am particularly interested in building AI systems that can support biomedical discovery, improve clinical interpretation, and help translate complex health data into actionable insights. My current research includes explainable AI frameworks for medical diagnosis, multimodal deep learning for pediatric ophthalmology, ontology-based feature engineering, and machine learning methods for biomarker discovery and clinical risk prediction.

Before my doctoral training, I worked in public health, nutrition, and health systems research in Nepal. This background continues to shape my interest in translational research that connects computational methods with real-world clinical and population health needs.

Research Interests

Areas of focus across AI, informatics, and translational health.

Trustworthy and Explainable AI in Medicine

I develop and apply explainable AI methods to improve transparency, interpretability, and clinical trust in machine learning and large language model-based systems.

Large Language Models for Clinical Decision Support

My research explores how LLMs can be evaluated, interpreted, and integrated into clinically meaningful workflows, especially where model explanations and safety are essential.

Multimodal Deep Learning for Biomedical Data

I work with eye movement recordings, clinical biomarkers, and structured health records to develop models that support diagnosis, prediction, and discovery.

Ontology-Driven Biomedical Informatics

I use biomedical ontologies and knowledge representation to improve feature engineering, data harmonization, and clinically interpretable machine learning workflows.

Translational Public Health and Population Health Informatics

My earlier work focused on maternal-child health, nutrition, healthcare access, and pandemic preparedness in low-resource settings.

Research Highlights

Selected threads from recent work.

Explainable AI and Large Language Models for Clinical Interpretation

My recent work investigates how explainable AI frameworks can be applied to large language models in medical diagnosis, including pediatric ophthalmology and other clinical contexts where transparency matters.

Multimodal AI for Pediatric Ophthalmology

I have developed and evaluated machine learning and deep learning approaches for early detection of amblyopia using eye movement recordings and multimodal clinical data.

Machine Learning Interpretability for Clinical Biomarkers

Another area of my work focuses on interpretable machine learning for biomarker discovery and risk prediction, with emphasis on hematologic biomarkers and clinical understanding.

Ontology-Based Feature Engineering and Biomedical Knowledge Representation

I also contribute to ontology-based feature engineering and semantic approaches for heterogeneous clinical data to support reusable and interpretable workflows.
